Public Sentiment

Tweets may give insight into how people feel about public transit

Click on a dot to see the polarity and subjectivity rating for each state’s tweets about public transit. A polarity score closer to +1 means the state was tweeting more positively, where a score closer to -1 means more negative tweets, and a score closer to 0 is neutral. A subjectivity score closer to 0 indicates objective tweets, where a score closer to +1 indicates subjective tweets.

Relationship between ridership & funding

The orange dots on this graph show the predicted ridership over the past 20 years based on urban transit funding, the previous year’s ridership, and the ridership from two years ago. The blue dots show actual ridership, demonstrating the relatively high accuracy of this model and its potential to predict future ridership based on these features.
